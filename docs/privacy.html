Sigil Messenger — Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 29, 2026

Data Collection

Sigil Messenger does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to our servers. There are no servers — Sigil is a peer-to-peer messenger built on the Nostr protocol.

Messages

All messages are end-to-end encrypted using NIP-04. Messages are transmitted through public Nostr relays but their content is encrypted and unreadable by relay operators.

Keys

Your cryptographic keypair is generated and stored locally on your device. We never have access to your private key.

Contacts

Your contact list is stored locally on your device using SwiftData. It is not uploaded to any server.

Analytics

Sigil does not include any analytics, tracking, or telemetry.

Third-Party Services

Sigil connects to public Nostr relays (e.g., relay.damus.io) to send and receive messages. These relays may log connection metadata (IP addresses, timestamps) per their own privacy policies.

Contact

For questions about this policy, open an issue at github.com/lmanchu/sigil.
